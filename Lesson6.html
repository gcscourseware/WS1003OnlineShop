LU06:Payment and Shipping Set-up

Establishing reliable transactions and clear customer expectations.

üí∞ 1. Securing Transactions with Payment Gateways

A payment gateway processes credit card details securely. You must choose a reliable provider to earn customer trust.

Platform-Based (e.g., Shopify Payments, Stripe): Easiest setup. The platform handles most compliance and security. Recommended for beginners.

Third-Party Gateways (e.g., PayPal, Square): Offer flexibility and trust but may involve slightly higher transaction fees or redirect customers off your site briefly during checkout.

Manual/Alternative: Bank transfers or Cash on Delivery (COD). Use sparingly as they increase friction and fraud risk.

When handling payment data, security is paramount. The industry standard is **PCI Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)**.

Crucial: Use SSL (HTTPS) on your entire website and ensure your chosen gateway is PCI compliant. If a customer's card data never touches your server (standard for hosted platforms), your compliance burden is minimal.

üì¶ 2. Defining Shipping Options and Policies

Shipping is a primary source of cart abandonment. Clear, transparent, and fair policies are non-negotiable.

You need to choose a method for charging customers:

  1. **Flat Rate:** A single price for shipping, regardless of order size. Simple, but can be inaccurate for heavy items.
  2. **Free Shipping:** Absorbing shipping cost into the product price. Great for marketing, often triggered by a minimum order value.
  3. **Carrier Calculated Rates:** Integrating with services (USPS, FedEx) to pull real-time costs based on package weight, size, and destination zip code. Highly accurate but requires precise product weights.

Shipping zones define the geographic regions you ship to (e.g., Local, National, International). Rates are set per zone.

Tip: Always include estimated *processing time* (e.g., 1-2 days to pack the order) separate from *transit time* (e.g., 3-5 days delivery).

ü§ù 3. Returns and Customer Support

A good returns policy builds trust and reduces anxiety for first-time buyers.

Your policy must clearly state:

  • **Return Window:** How many days the customer has to return the item (e.g., 30 days from delivery).
  • **Condition:** Must the item be unworn, unopened, or in its original packaging?
  • **Refund Method:** Store credit, exchange, or original payment refund?
  • **Cost:** Who pays for return shipping (usually the customer, unless the item was damaged/faulty)?

Define your communication channels and your commitment to response times (Service Level Agreement or SLA).

Channels: Email (required), Live Chat (high conversion), Phone (optional, high cost).

SLA Example: "We respond to all email inquiries within 24 business hours." Communicate this promise clearly on your contact page.
